Our priests Mpho and Kyle are helping us figure out what it means to walk the talk of Jesus.

We believe there's a place in Christianity for our busy, modern, not-so-sure-about-it-all lives. Since late 2022, we've been gathering in Amsterdam to explore what it means for us to come together in the name of Jesus, and bring our whole selves when we do.

After deciding we were ready for the next step on the fourth of July 2023, we've become a mission congregation of the Convocation of Episcopal Churches in Europe.

At All Saints, each of us is finding ways to show up with whatever we have today, whether that be stories of hardship or stories of pure joy. We find inspiration, consolation, and challenge in rite and in Scripture. We love coming together during engaging, intimate gatherings.

We wholeheartedly believe that real life doesn't stop when we walk into church, and that faith doesn't stop when we walk back out. Questions like "how do I navigate this geopolitical conflict?", "how can I be a good ally?", "how do I overcome my religious trauma?" and "where am I selling myself and my loved ones short?" easily find their way into our conversations. Working through them together is how we remind ourselves we're not alone.

Good to know

  • Our services are in English Although we like to sprinkle in other languages as well
  • Children are welcome No need to call your babysitter, let your kid(s) join ours! We alternate child-centered services and separate children's activities.
  • We're wheelchair accessible Our church is equipped with free seating and as well as ramps and an elevator to make sure you can find your way around.
  • We're very serious about social justice Our priest Mpho is the Canon for Racial Justice and Beloved Community for the Episcopal Church Europe.
